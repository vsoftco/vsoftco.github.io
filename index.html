About

I am the CEO and President of softwareQ Inc, and an Affiliate of the Institute for Quantum Computing.

I am involved in the CryptoWorks21 Quantum-Safe Cryptographic Infrastructure Program and I am a member of the European Telecommunications Standards Institute (ETSI) Quantum-Safe Cryptography Standardization Group.

I graduated from Carnegie Mellon University with a PhD in Theoretical Physics. My advisor was Robert B. Griffiths.

Research

I am interested in quantum software and quantum architectures, applications of small-sized quantum computers (NISQ) to concrete optimization problems, fault-tolerant quantum processing, quantum communication, quantum and classical cryptography and classical/quantum separation.

Referee service

Software

My personal GitHub repositories
The Quantum Software group at IQC

I contribute to open source software. Below is a list of some of my favourite projects/code snippets I developed:

  • Quantum++
    – A modern C++11 general purpose quantum computing library, composed solely of template header files. It uses the Eigen 3 linear algebra library and, if available, the OpenMP multi-processing library. Status: active, version 3.0 released on 5 October 2021 under the MIT License.

  • I am a proud contributor to the Open Quantum Safe liboqs PQC library.

  • liboqs-cpp
    – A C++11 wrapper around Open Quantum Safe liboqs PQC library.

  • liboqs-go
    – A Go wrapper around Open Quantum Safe liboqs PQC library.

  • I am a contributor to liboqs-python
    – A Python 3 wrapper around Open Quantum Safe liboqs PQC library.

  • Design patterns
    – My take on design patterns in modern C++ (C++11/C++14).

  • spell
    – Tired of (mis)spelling your name during a phone call? spell.py does it for you, using phonetic alphabet(s) (NATO phonetic alphabet by default).

  • pytexnumber
    – A Python 2/3 console program for renumbering LaTeX references (labels).

  • cpptexnumber
    – The C++11 version of pytexnumber above.

  • Texnumber / GTexnumber
    – A console/GUI Java program for renumbering LaTeX references (labels). The Java sources are included in the .jar files. The Netbeans project (including sources) is located here.
    • Description: same as pytexnumber above.

    • Usage: Unzip the archive then run the program with
        java -jar [G]Texnumber.jar
